Operating standard

Principles that protect useful collaboration

✓

Enablement, not dependency

The network should keep moving without waiting for one central person.

✓

Trust with evidence

Capabilities and market access should be supported by prior work, references, or measurable results.

✓

Tangible value

Convert open discussion into a product, pilot, package, or measurable business outcome.

✓

Portfolio, not one project

Maintain multiple opportunities at different stages instead of closing the community around one idea.

✓

Follow-up is part of the product

An introduction is incomplete until the outcome returns to the shared knowledge system.

✓

Privacy by design

Publish sanitized knowledge while keeping personal contacts and sensitive discussion outside the public site.

Next meeting runbook

Turn the second meeting into an execution session

Select no more than three opportunities and leave with owners, evidence gaps, experiments, and review dates.

Before

  1. Collect structured member profiles.
  2. Capture capabilities, evidence, markets, offers, and asks.
  3. Publish a short opportunity queue.
  4. Confirm transcript and publication consent.

During

  1. Use five-minute capability statements.
  2. Select up to three opportunities.
  3. Assign opportunity, domain, market, and research leads.
  4. Record decisions, objections, evidence gaps, and experiments.

After

  1. Publish a sanitized record within 24 hours.
  2. Schedule purpose-specific bilateral introductions.
  3. Review progress after 7 and 14 days.
  4. Move only evidence-backed ideas into pilot planning.
