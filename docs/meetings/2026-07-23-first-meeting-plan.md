## 8. Operating principles extracted from the meeting

- **Enablement, not dependency:** the community should not stop when one central person is unavailable.
- **Decentralized collaboration:** encourage direct member-to-member meetings and preserve their outcomes.
- **Trust with evidence:** profiles and claims should be supported by previous work, references, or market results.
- **Tangible value:** members respond better to a concrete product, pilot, package, or measurable outcome than to open-ended discussion.
- **Opportunity diversity:** do not close the group around one project; maintain a portfolio of opportunities.
- **Follow-up is part of the product:** an introduction without an owner and next step is not a completed outcome.
- **Privacy by design:** publish sanitized knowledge; keep personal contact details and sensitive conversations outside the public site.

## 9. Next meeting runbook

### Before the meeting

- Collect a structured one-page profile from every participant.
- Ask each member for: capabilities, evidence, sectors, assets, markets, offers, and current asks.
- Publish a short opportunity queue before the call.
- Confirm whether transcript notes may be used and at what publication level.

### During the meeting

- Five-minute capability statements, not long biographies.
- Select no more than three opportunities for active discussion.
- For each opportunity assign: opportunity owner, domain lead, market lead, and research lead.
- Record decisions, objections, evidence gaps, and next experiments.

### After the meeting

- Publish the sanitized meeting intelligence page within 24 hours.
- Schedule bilateral introductions with an explicit purpose.
- Review the opportunity queue after 7 and 14 days.
- Move only evidence-backed opportunities into pilot planning.
